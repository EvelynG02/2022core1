Sativa

  • Cannabis Sativa originates in dry and hot climates such as those found in Central America, Africa, and Southeast Asia. The plants are thin and tall, with narrow leaves.
  • Sativa strains are higher in THC, the euphoria-inducing component in cannabis, and lower in CBD. They have an energizing, stimulating, mentally invigorating effect known to improve focus and clarity. Sativas are usually recommended for morning or daytime use.
  • Some examples of popular sativa strains are Haze Berry, Acapulco Gold, and Sour Diesel.

Hybrid

  • Hybrid strains are crosses between various indica and sativa breeds. Hybrid strain qualities depend on the properties of the parent plants. Cannabis growers are always busy producing new strains to achieve desired effects.
  • The percentage of THC and CBD in a hybrid plant will depend on whether the strain is sativa-dominant, indica-dominant, or balanced. Some examples of well-known hybrid strains are Blue Dream, Wedding Cake, and Gelato.

Indica

  • Cannabis Indica hails from India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, and Turkey. Indica plants are short and bushy, with broad chunky leaves. Indicas are faster growers and more prolific producers than sativas.
  • Indica strains are lower in THC and higher in CBD, which makes them especially rich in medicinal benefits. They have a soothing, relaxing, sleep-inducing effect and are perfect for evening use.
  • Popular indica strains include Granddaddy Purple, Hindu Kush, and Blue Cheese.
